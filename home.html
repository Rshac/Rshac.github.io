The Gospel

The word "gospel" is the translation of a Greek word which means "good news". It's referring to the good news of Jesus, and what He did for us all. Jesus died to save us from the penalty of our sin. What does that mean? To answer that, we have to know what the bad news is. The bad news is the reality of hell. God is perfectly just and must punish sin. "For the wages of sin is death, but the free gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord" (Ro 6:23). Every person has sinned, so every person deserves punishment (Ro 3:23). But God created us for fellowship with Him, and He has made a way for us to still have that. The good news is that Jesus endured the full wrath of the Father poured out on Him, including separation from the Father. (Mt 27:46) He took our sin and offers us His righteousness instead (2 Cor 5:21). Now, if you'll "repent and believe" you will be saved (Mark 1:15). We encourage you to do this, to make Jesus the Lord of your life, and let His transforming grace change you.
